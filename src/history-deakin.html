Major Projects

SumoBot

A robot designed for "fighting" in a circular arena, with the intention of pushing an opponent out of the ring without being pushed out itself. The robot was designed, built and programmed by me. It looks a bit messy, but it worked!

The robot makes use of multiple sensors to detect objects and arena boundaries. Primarily ultrasonic sensors are used for object detection in multiple directions, and line sensors on each edge of the robot detect the arena boundary. Differential steering is used to control the robot.

Arduino

Snake Robot

For my final year engineering project, I designed, built and programmed a robot with snake-like sinusoidal movement. The robot is made up of multiple identical segments each with its own controller, servo motor and power source. The servo positions are determined by the master controller in the head and communicated to the other controllers via an I2C connection.

One of my aims for this project was to improve on a previous iteration of the snake robot by another student. Thanks in part to the comparitively larger wheels this robot demonstrated a greater degree of forward movement, but there is still a lot of room for improvement.

Internship - Teleroo

In order to complete my course, I undertook a 12-week internship with 2mar Robotics, where I got to work with a prototype of their telepresence robot (now called Teleport). I helped fine-tune the movement controls as well as add features such as sensors for obstacle detection. I also helped with the electrical assembly and with developing an online interface for users of the product.
